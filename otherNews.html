The City Clock

read more about The City Clock here

Developed by a french couple who drew up the idea while observing lit buildings in Paris one night, the City Clock is not your ordinary clock. In fact, people may not even believe it to be a clock upon first glance. The clock lights up certain windows of the building every time a minute goes by following a binary pattern:

 the top window is +8 (2³).

 the third window up is +4 (2²)

 the second window up is +2 (2¹)

 The bottom window is +1

Military time is taken into account for having windows light up, so 5:03 in the afternoon would be 17:03 in this format. Then, for each of the four numbers of the time, the windows are lit up from left to right dependent on the number in time shown.

Clearly, finding the time is a more challenging process here, but this clock is nevertheless a neat object to put on display. As an alarm clock, however...not so much.
