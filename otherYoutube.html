When it was introduced at WWDC in 2007, the iPhone was hyped up to be revolutionary to the mobile phone marketplace. Serving as "A phone, an iPod, and an internet communicator", the iPhone's versatility and simplicity cemented its position as a premier choice in the cell phone industry. Eliminating the need for a plastic keyboard with a substitution of a touch-screen display, the iPhone felt like a work of magic.

But have you wondered how the iPhone is put together? In this video, American engineer, entrepreneur, and hacker Scotty Allen (youtube: Strange Parts) is traveling in and out of China on business and comes across small, independently-run shops with owners selling individual parts for cell phones and other electronics. With an innate sense of curiosity, Allen dives in with both feet, spending months of his time and hundreds of dollars buying the necessary parts and assembling his own iPhone 6S. He runs into some obstacles during the assembling process, but is able to push through with the help of some friendly local shopowners.
